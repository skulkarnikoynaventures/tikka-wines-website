Velvet Truffle

An Ice Wine Blend for Mithai, Saffron, Rose, and Celebration

South Asian celebrations are not complete without sweets.

Gulab jamun, rasmalai, kaju katli, peda, jalebi, shrikhand, rabri, kulfi, and saffron desserts all carry rich sweetness, dairy, nuts, rose, cardamom, and spice.

These desserts need a wine that is not merely sweet—but balanced, luxurious, and intense.

Velvet Truffle is the celebratory expression of the Tikka Wines portfolio: an ice wine blend created for dessert, gifting, weddings, festivals, and after-dinner luxury.

The name evokes texture, richness, and indulgence. It is designed to feel premium, sensuous, and ceremonial.

Background & Origin

Ice wine styles are traditionally associated with cold-climate regions where grapes freeze naturally on the vine, concentrating sugars and flavors before pressing.

For Tikka Wines, Velvet Truffle can be positioned around:

  • Luxury
  • Dessert pairing
  • Gifting
  • Wedding tables
  • Festival occasions
  • Premium after-dinner service

It is not an everyday table wine. It is a finishing wine—a wine for the last course, the special toast, the plated dessert, or the gift box.

Why It Works with South Asian Desserts

South Asian desserts are often:

  • Milk-based
  • Nut-based
  • Saffron-scented
  • Rose-scented
  • Cardamom-rich
  • Syrup-soaked
  • Dense or creamy

A good dessert wine must have enough sweetness to match the dessert, but also enough acidity to avoid becoming cloying.

Velvet Truffle is designed for exactly that kind of balance: sweet enough for mithai, bright enough to refresh the palate, and luxurious enough to feel worthy of the occasion.

Serving Recommendation

Serve very well chilled in small pours, around 38–42°F. Velvet Truffle is best treated as a luxury finishing wine, not a casual table wine.

Pair it with: Mithai, saffron desserts, rose-cardamom sweets, luxury gifting
Choose Velvet Truffle when: the occasion deserves ceremony.
