Silver Trunk White

A Gewürztraminer-Based White for Masala, Heat, and Fragrance

Silver Trunk White is built around one of the most compelling wine styles for South Asian cuisine: Gewürztraminer.

This style speaks the same aromatic language as the food. It is expressive, floral, lush, and spice-friendly. Its signature notes often include lychee, rose, tropical fruit, ginger, and warm spice. These characteristics make it especially compatible with dishes that use cardamom, clove, cinnamon, saffron, coriander, ginger, and creamy sauces.

For Tikka Wines, Silver Trunk White is not simply an aromatic white. It is the wine we imagine for the first moment of discovery—the bottle that helps a South Asian household realize that wine can work beautifully with familiar food.

Background & Origin

Gewürztraminer is most famously associated with Alsace in France, though it is also grown in Germany, Austria, Italy, New World regions, and select cooler-climate vineyards.

The grape is known for intensity, perfume, and texture. It is often described as floral, exotic, and spice-leaning. These features make it highly relevant to South Asian cuisine, where aromatics are central to the experience.

The name Silver Trunk White gives the wine a distinctive Tikka Wines identity: elegant, memorable, and subtly connected to the brand’s playful animal imagery while still feeling premium.

Why It Works with South Asian Food

Best with:

  • Creamy gravies
  • Tandoori dishes
  • Mild to medium spice
  • Saffron and cardamom dishes
  • Paneer-based meals
  • Warm spice profiles

Pairing logic:

  • Floral notes echo cardamom, rose, and saffron
  • Tropical fruit balances chili and ginger
  • Slight sweetness softens heat
  • Round texture works with cream, yogurt, ghee, and nut-based sauces
  • Aromatic lift prevents rich dishes from feeling heavy

Suggested Pairings

North Indian

  • Chicken tikka masala
  • Butter chicken
  • Paneer butter masala
  • Malai kofta
  • Navratan korma

Tandoori & Grilled

  • Paneer tikka

  • Tandoori cauliflower

Biryani & Rice

  • Chicken biryani with raita

Vegetarian

  • Saag paneer
  • Methi malai mutter
  • Shahi paneer

Serving Recommendation

Serve chilled, but not ice-cold. Around 45–50°F is ideal for preserving aromatics while keeping the wine refreshing.

Pair it with: Creamy curries, tandoori plates, paneer dishes
Choose Silver Trunk White when: the food is aromatic, creamy, and moderately spicy.
